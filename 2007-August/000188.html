> I'm new in embedded systems (so please be kind with me even if my question

> I was surpised by  the function vcan_tx.

> My questions are:
> _ why isn't there any function like netif_tx for the effective socket buffer
> transmission? I noticed that there is the function netif_rx for reception.

the counterpart to netif_rx ist the hard_start_xmit in the struct
net_dev [1]

> _ how is the packet, let's say physically, sent?

vcan.c is the virtual can device, thus a packet is never send
physically. But If you have a real can-driver the packet is send in the
hard_start_xmit function.

> _ does the CAN core manage transmission elsewhere? And in this case where
> and how?

nope..
