>
>  The SocketCAN approach uses the Linux networking subsystem to work with CAN
> as any other networking does. Therefore the device driver model is a network
> device driver (like your ethernet card) and NOT a character device (like e.g
> a serial adaptor or unfortunately the current Kvaser driver).
>
>  Due to the netdevice driver model the CAN interface is always similar to
> the application, which allows to run the same application on different CAN
> hardware. Of course you also won't be happy to reprogramm your web-browser
> when you change your Ethernet card in your system ;-)
>
>  Programming a netdevice driver is in most cases easier than handling all
> the FIFO stuff and different APIs yourself when programming a character
> device. Although we need to wait for the Kvaser driver supporting the
> netdevice driver modell ... as it is open source you are heavily invited to
> modify it :-)
>  Or just give the developers a hint about the coming CAN subsystem in the
> Linux 2.6.25 kernel.
>
