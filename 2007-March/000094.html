Indeed all the ioctl() stuff is an open point to me. I would suggest to 
get the PF_CAN core into the Mainstream Kernel and to work on the 
(missing items of the) CAN-netdriver interface in parallel. The big 
amount of typedefs that's currently planned in linux/can/ioctl.h is 
definitely not Kernel style.

The only thing that is fixed is the discussed content in 
linux/can/error.h (the definition of the error frame). Regarding your 
question, i would /assume/ the SIOCGCANERRORCONFIG to be the 
configuration for the CAN-driver. E.g. which parts of the errorframe to 
be supported by the driver.
