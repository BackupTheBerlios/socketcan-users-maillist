Hi all,

i uploaded the (assumed) final SVG files to the SocketCAN project files 
on BerliOS:
https://developer.berlios.de/project/showfiles.php?group_id=6475

Both files base on the standard Tux e.g. from 
http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/35/Tux.svg

This one has a shadow below the (standard) Tux:
http://prdownload.berlios.de/socketcan/SocketCAN-logo.svg

And this one has no shadow below the (modified) Tux:
http://prdownload.berlios.de/socketcan/SocketCAN-logo-noshadow.svg

Both SVG Files are under Creative Commons License (following the license 
of the original Tux.svg) as stated in the SVGs meta data. So you can do 
whatever you want with the logo as long as you mention the Authors/ 
/Larry Ewing, Simon Budig, Anja Gerwinski and now also me :-)

Logo changes since the last RFC following the given feedback:

- reduced colors to already used colors (wires in yellow/black with a 
gray CAN logo)
- the Tux has standard yellow feet again (and the shades)
- the wires are really twisted (black and yellow switch in foreground)
- the wires have been created with new bezier curves (Thanks to Andre 
Naujoks for the Inkscape assistance)

I created many similar logos with different CAN logo and CAN wire colors 
(CiA green, CiA blue, black, gray, whatever) and the gray CAN logo with 
yellow/black wires was voted best by a colleague of mine - a real 
industrial designer. And i felt he was right ;-)

The logo hopefully fit's all (y)our needs now.

Have fun!

Oliver
