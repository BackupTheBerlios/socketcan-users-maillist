If you type Ctrl-C here, the candump will terminate and you would be 
able to see the received frame with:

cat candump-1970-01-01_005122.log

But only when there was one, which i do not expect, regarding the packet 
counters you have shown with 'ifconfig' ...

Btw. i would be much better, when you just open *two* shells on your 
Box, and say

'candump any' in the first shell and

'cansend can0 150#ABCDEF' in the second shell.


And maybe you should try the (surely working) vcan driver to get 
familiar with these tools and the data flow:

modprobe vcan
ifconfig vcan0 up

and then make the first steps with it.
