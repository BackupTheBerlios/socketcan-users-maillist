René Müller wrote:
> I found in the documentation for Socketcan a chapter about transport 
> protocols. There are some manufacture specific protocols (VAG TP1.6, 
> VAG TP2.0, ...) and the ISO-TP. In the documentation is stated, that 
> VW is working on ISO-TP and wants to publish it under the BSD/GPL. How 
> is the status of this? Is there an implementation available?

Hi René,

indeed i'm working on it (currently only in my spare time) and have just 
designed the receive path, which is fortunately pretty easy. The design 
follows the UDP approach which is also an unreliable datagram protocol 
like ISO-TP.

My main concern is the 'direct feedback' to the application in the case 
of failed PDU transmits, which are defined in the ISO15765-2 but do not 
fit into the commonly known 'unreliable datagram' design.

And having a blocking write for each PDU is also crude ... so i'm not 
yet finished with the tx path. Finally  ISO-TP is a common standard and 
therefore it can/will go into kernel under GPL.

Are you currently working with ISO-TP? Maybe you can give some feedback 
on my approach or you'd like to be an early adopter ;-)

Regards,
Oliver
