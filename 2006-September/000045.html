Syed Amer Gilani wrote:
> I have an MPC5200B an a board from Phytec. Running on it is a 2.6.17
> Linux with socket-can.

> To test CAN i connected it to a CAN Monitor from stzp. When i now send
> something with "cansend can0 --identifier=0x20 0xAA" the CAN Monitor
> doesn't receive anything at all.
> After some time my Console gets spammed with :

> NETDEV WATCHDOG: can0: transmit timed out
> mscan_tx_timeout

> Does somebody has a hint what i could be doing wrong?

Is this your CAN-Net propperly terminated? Are the resistors set in the 
right direction to support CAN?

Do you have a valid receiver on this net? Your sender keeps sending a 
packet till it get's ACKed by at least one station. If your CAN Monitor 
is passive the first message never leaves the transmitter.

BTW:
The OSELAS.BSP() that comes with your board uses the old, stable 
socket-can version. Here on berlios is the current development tree. 
Please come to Oselas Mailinglist to discuss BSP related issues.

http://www.pengutronix.de/oselas/community/index_en.html
