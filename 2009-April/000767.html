CAN_BCM is the protocol number inside the protocol family PF_CAN.

It is defined in can.h - or more precisely in linux-2.6.25/include/linux/can.h

On my new debian system the file can.h already exists in

/usr/include/linux/can.h

But when this is not available in your system, i would suggest to define the
place of your CAN includes in your compiler flags, like in the

socketcan/trunk/can-utils/Makefile:

CFLAGS    = -O2 -Wall -Wno-parentheses -I../kernel/2.6/include \
            -fno-strict-aliasing \
            -DPF_CAN=29 \
            -DAF_CAN=PF_CAN

Instead of "-I../kernel/2.6/include" you might also use

-I/home/<user>/socketcan/trunk/kernel/2.6/include

or

-I/home/<user>/<my-linux-2.6.??-tree>/include

The definitions are always defined the same.
