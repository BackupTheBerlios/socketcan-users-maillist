Well, in that case you know what you have to do in order to improve the
situation. Tell us what you want to have in your BSP and you'll get it.
I don't see the problem with that.

We have a need for cross compilable userspace tools for SocketCAN since
2005, there had been none at that time out there who have been
maintained on a regular base, with well defined releases against
in-kernel patch stacks etc. So we made a package of our own, in order to
solve real problems. They have been maintained since 2005 and released
on a regular base, they work with all releases we have out there (up to
2.6.26 so far) and they do what they are supposed to in their respective
usecases. We have several tests in our automatic test suites based upon
them, so they solve *our* problems. Nobody said that it works for
everyone else out there. This is Open Source, anyone can do what solves
his problems.

We agree on the fact that things should be unified and this discussion
is completely silly. You told me "why don't you do it" and I told you
that we will do when we either have project time or too much spare time.

It's really that simple.
