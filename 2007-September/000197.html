juanba romance wrote:
> Hello all, i am currently developing a driver for the mcf5282 Flex
> canbus device under uClinux and i have a question related with the
> .dlc field management
> i have verified that the current drivers (sja100/mscan) update the
> typical " stats.tx_bytes" counter field based on the dlc  "can_frame"
> input value.
> Our application defines  messages with the zeroed dlc, and of course
> they are still considered as "network packets"
> so it's a bit confusing to monitor the physical device and see that
> the tx count doesn't have in care these ones.
>
> Is this a general policy of the socket-can stack ?

Hi,

if you look around in the Linux kernel, you'll see that this is a
networking policy in general.

stats.tx_packets : counts the transmitted packets
stats.tx_bytes : counts the transmitted packet payload in bytes

So if your (not very usual) use-case mostly works without any payload
(what can_frame.data[] definitely is) you probably need to 'pimp' your
networking monitor to display and count the packets and not only the
payload ...

I assume your network monitor to be open source :-)
