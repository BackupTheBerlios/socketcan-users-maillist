> There can be quite a long list of tx timeouts afterwards, but it will
> stop after some while.
>
> What does a tx timeout in CAN actually mean ?

The NETDEV WATCHDOG stuff is a general functionality provided by the 
Linux kernel to check whether a (successful) TX interrupt confirms the 
successful sending of a frame. In the case of this NETDEV WATCHDOG the 
send request in the CAN controller has to be canceled.

The overflow of the Tx Ring looks like a problem in locking and filling 
the mscan TX ring somewhere in the mscan driver. Maybe Andrey Volkov or 
Wolfgang Grandegger know more about this issue.
