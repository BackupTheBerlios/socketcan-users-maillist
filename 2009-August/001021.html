Hi Tom,

I worked with the ELM327 some times ago. First, it is not an CAN
interface, but an OBD to RS232 Interpreter. So you can do some basic
diagnostic with your car, but as far as I can remember no pure CAN
communication. The ELM327 just takes your diagnostic request, sends it
to the car (over CAN, J1850 or K-Line) and presents you the diagnostic
response over RS232.

I can remember an listen only mode, in which you can put the ELM327.
Then it will send all received CAN frames to RS232. I'm not sure if you
can send any frames not related to diagnostics with the ELM327.

Hope this helps,
René

Hi,

I'm wondering if Socket CAN supports the ELM327 interface? It's a
combined OBDII and CAN interface available for US$30 at Deal Extreme
http://www.dealextreme.com/details.dx/sku.28528

The full hardware and host PC protocol are available at
http://www.elmelectronics.com/DSheets/ELM327DS.pdf The protocol is AT
command based. From what little I understand about Socket CAN, it might
work with the slcan driver after you send the appropriate AT commands to
configure a compatible mode?

Has anyone tried this interface?
