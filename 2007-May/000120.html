On Mon, May 21, 2007 at 07:59:28PM +0200, Oliver Hartkopp wrote:
> indeed the socketcan stuff provided in the OSELAS toolchain is derived 
> from a very old SocketCAN version.

OSELAS.Toolchain() provides - as the name suggests - toolchains. That's
gcc and friends. Has nothing to do with CAN at all.

> There is no local loopback functionality and e.g. the basic struct
> can_frame differs a lot. We had many discussions on the socket-API, on
> filters of RAW-sockets and on these structs which lead to the current
> SocketCAN which is here on BerliOS.

He asked about the ccan core in the h720x, so I assume the hint to the
driver in the phyCORE-MPC5200B-tiny BSP is a good one.

Note that, although the h720x has a standard ccan, he hardware designers
didn't notice that it is not enough to just connect the data and address
bus of two chips - you also need to take care of boring things like
timings. So what you basically have to do is to do a bus access, wait
some cycles and do the same bus access again. Braindamaged, but works :)
But don't expect the best performance since sliced bread.

Integrating that into the berlios tree is left as a finger exercise to
the casual observer.
