> The C164 is used as can controller. It comes with a firmware from Infineon.
> The communication with this firmware is made by a dual-port memory.

These kind of interfaces are known from so-called 'active' CAN interface
cards. Sometimes the onboard CPUs on these 'active' CAN interfaces require a
firmware that's loaded at CAN interface initialization.

Examples for 'active' DPRAM CAN interface cards with SocketCAN support are:

some Softing Cards (like EDIC, CANcard-2, etc. - needs firmware)

some ESD Cards (like the esd_pci331 driver - has onboard firmware)

So when you need some ideas how to attach your C164 via DPRAM, looking into
the esd_pci331 driver is definitely a good starting point.
